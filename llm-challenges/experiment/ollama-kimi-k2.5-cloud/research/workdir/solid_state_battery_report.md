# Solid State Battery Status Report: Late 2024/2025

**Report Date:** March 29, 2026  
**Coverage Period:** Late 2024 - 2025

---

## Executive Summary

Solid-state batteries (SSBs) have moved from laboratory curiosity to pre-commercial reality in 2024-2025. While **mass-market EVs with true all-solid-state batteries are not yet available**, significant milestones have been achieved including real-world vehicle testing, validation by major automakers, and announced production timelines. The industry has shifted from "if" to "when"—with most major players targeting 2027-2030 for commercialization.

## 3. Main Technical Problems Still Being Solved

### Manufacturing & Scalability Challenges

#### **1. Solid Electrolyte Production**
- **Challenge:** Producing uniform, defect-free solid electrolytes at scale
- **Types in development:** Sulfide, oxide, and polymer-based electrolytes
- **Issue:** Each type has trade-offs in conductivity, stability, and manufacturing cost
- **Progress:** Dry-electrode manufacturing lines being developed

#### **2. Interface Resistance**
- **Problem:** Poor contact between solid electrolyte and electrodes reduces performance
- **Impact:** Limits fast-charging capability and energy efficiency
- **Solutions:** Advanced coatings, pressure management systems, interface engineering

#### **3. Dendrite Formation**
- **Issue:** Lithium metal dendrites can grow through solid electrolytes, causing short circuits
- **Criticality:** Major safety concern for lithium-metal anode designs
- **Progress:** Ceramic separators and novel electrolyte compositions showing promise

#### **4. Cell Expansion & Mechanical Stress**
- **Problem:** Silicon-rich and lithium-metal anodes expand during charging, causing mechanical stress
- **Example:** Enovix using stainless-steel constraint systems to minimize swelling to <2%
- **Challenge:** Managing expansion over thousands of cycles

### Cost Barriers

| Company | Cost Target | Current Status |
|---------|-------------|----------------|
| **Nissan** | $75/kWh by 2028 ($65 thereafter) | Prototype phase |
| **Solid Power** | ~$85/kWh | Pilot production |
| **Industry Average** | $130-150/kWh (est.) | Semi-solid state |

**Cost Challenge:** True all-solid-state batteries currently cost significantly more than conventional lithium-ion.

### Performance Optimization

#### **Energy Density Progress:**
- **Current Semi-Solid:** 180-375 Wh/kg (in vehicles)
- **Prototype All-Solid:** 350-500 Wh/kg
- **Target:** 500+ Wh/kg commercially

#### **Fast Charging:**
- **Achieved:** 15-90% in 18 minutes (Stellantis/Factorial, room temperature)
- **Claimed:** 10-80% in 6.5 minutes (Rimac/ProLogium prototype, 100 kWh)
- **Challenge:** Maintaining cycle life with ultra-fast charging

#### **Temperature Range:**
- **Stellantis/Factorial:** -22°F to 113°F (-30°C to 45°C) operating range
- **Challenge:** Solid electrolytes often have limited low-temperature performance

### Supply Chain & Materials

1. **Lithium Metal Anodes:** Limited production capacity for battery-grade lithium metal
2. **Solid Electrolyte Materials:** Sulfide-based materials require moisture-free environments
3. **Dry Processing Equipment:** New manufacturing equipment needed for dry electrode coating
4. **Quality Control:** Higher defect sensitivity than liquid electrolyte cells

## Conclusion

**The Reality Check:** Solid-state batteries are no longer "10 years away"—they are **2-5 years from limited commercialization**. However, several caveats apply:

1. **Semi-Solid State First:** Most near-term deployments use semi-solid-state (gel-like electrolyte) rather than true all-solid-state
2. **Premium Vehicles First:** Initial launches will be in luxury/performance EVs, not mass-market models
3. **China Leads Deployment:** 83% of manufacturing capacity is Chinese
4. **Cost Premium Expected:** Early solid-state EVs will carry significant cost premiums

**Bottom Line:** Expect to see small-scale production EVs with solid-state batteries from **Factorial (2027), Toyota (2027-28), and Samsung SDI (2027)**. Mass-market adoption likely remains a **2028-2030** prospect.
