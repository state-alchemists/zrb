# Solid-State Battery Status Report: Late 2024/2025

**Report Date:** March 2026  
**Focus Period:** Late 2024 - 2025

---

## Executive Summary

Solid-state batteries (SSBs) have moved from laboratory research to early commercial deployment, with 2024 marking a turning point as Chinese manufacturers began delivering vehicles with semi-solid-state batteries. The technology promises 50% higher energy density, faster charging, and improved safety compared to conventional lithium-ion batteries. However, full solid-state batteries for mass-market EVs remain 2-3 years away, with major automakers targeting 2027-2028 for widespread deployment.

## 3. Main Technical Challenges

### Critical Technical Hurdles

Despite significant progress, several major challenges remain before widespread adoption:

#### 3.1 Solid-Solid Interface Resistance

**The Problem:** Unlike liquid electrolytes that "wet" all surfaces, solid electrolytes only make contact at discrete points with electrodes. This creates:
- High internal resistance
- Poor ion transfer kinetics
- Degraded battery performance over time

**Current Status:** This is considered *the most significant technical challenge* in solid-state battery development.

#### 3.2 Lithium Dendrite Formation

**The Problem:** 
- Lithium metal anodes can form needle-like structures (dendrites) that penetrate the solid electrolyte
- Causes internal short circuits and potential safety hazards
- Filament growth and fractures caused by stress gradients and defects

**Quote (Physics Today):** *"The use of a lithium-metal anode in SSBs is hindered by filament growth and fractures caused by stress gradients and defects."*

#### 3.3 Low Ionic Conductivity at Room Temperature

**The Problem:**
- Many solid electrolytes have significantly lower ionic conductivity than liquid electrolytes at room temperature
- Oxide-based electrolytes like LLZO require high sintering temperatures for dense microstructures
- This limits power density and charging speed, undermining key advantages

#### 3.4 Electrolyte Stability

**The Problem:**
- Solid electrolytes must remain chemically stable across wide voltage windows
- Degradation occurs at interfaces with both electrodes
- Long-term stability issues over hundreds of charge cycles remain unresolved

#### 3.5 Manufacturing & Scaling Challenges

**Key Issues:**
1. **"Production Hell"** - Transitioning from lab to mass production is extremely difficult
2. Long climb from laboratory validation to engineering validation
3. High-temperature processing requirements for oxide electrolytes
4. Maintaining consistent quality in large-format cells
5. Supply chain for specialized materials not yet established

#### 3.6 Cost and Economic Viability

**Current Barriers:**
- Manufacturing costs significantly higher than lithium-ion
- Limited economies of scale
- Complex production processes requiring new equipment
- Semi-solid-state technology (intermediate solution) may prove commercially viable before full SSBs

| Challenge | Severity | Progress Level |
|-----------|----------|----------------|

## 4. Technology Types: Semi-Solid vs. Full Solid-State

### Semi-Solid-State Batteries (Near-Term Solution)

Many companies are commercializing **semi-solid-state batteries** as an intermediate step:

**Advantages:**
- Easier to manufacture with existing technology
- Some safety improvements over traditional lithium-ion
- Higher energy density than conventional batteries
- Already in production (SAIC, NIO, GAC)

**How It Works:** Uses a small amount of liquid electrolyte combined with solid components, making manufacturing more compatible with existing lithium-ion production lines.

### Full All-Solid-State Batteries (Long-Term Goal)

**Characteristics:**
- Completely solid electrolyte (no liquid)
- Maximum safety benefits
- Highest theoretical energy density
- Most technically challenging to produce

**Timeline:** Mass production targeted 2027-2030+ depending on company

## 6. Outlook Summary

### What's Changed in 2024-2025:

✅ **From Lab to Road:** Real vehicles now shipping with semi-solid-state batteries  
✅ **Giga-scale Manufacturing:** First dedicated production facilities online  
✅ **Multiple OEM Commitments:** Major automakers have specific timelines  
✅ **Technology Convergence:** Semi-solid-state proving viable intermediate solution  

### What Hasn't Changed:

⚠️ **Full Solid-State Still Distant:** Mass production still 2-4 years away  
⚠️ **Technical Challenges Persist:** Interface resistance, dendrites, scaling  
⚠️ **Cost Premium:** Significant price premium over lithium-ion expected initially  
⚠️ **Infrastructure Gaps:** Manufacturing supply chain immature  

### Key Takeaways:

1. **2024-2025 was a turning point** - technology moved from perpetual "5 years away" to actual commercial deployment (at least for semi-solid-state)
2. **Chinese companies are leading** the early commercial push with production vehicle deployments
3. **Full solid-state batteries** remain on track for 2027-2028 mass production with Toyota leading the timeline
4. **Technical challenges are being solved incrementally**, not all at once
5. **Semi-solid-state batteries** represent a practical near-term solution while full SSB technology matures
