# Meet Zrb-Flow: AI Automation for the Command Line, Built for Docker and Kubernetes

Today, we’re launching **Zrb-Flow** — a new AI-powered automation tool for CLI-first teams who want faster workflows, fewer brittle scripts, and more reliable infrastructure operations.

If your day lives in terminals, containers, and clusters, Zrb-Flow is designed for you.

## What is Zrb-Flow?

Zrb-Flow brings intelligent automation directly into the command-line experience. It plugs into the environments modern engineers already use — especially **Docker** and **Kubernetes** — and helps turn repetitive, error-prone operational work into streamlined, adaptive flows.

Instead of treating automation like a pile of static scripts waiting to break, Zrb-Flow adds AI-driven decision-making to the process. That means your tooling can respond to real conditions, recover from common failures, and keep work moving without constant manual intervention.

## Built for the way infrastructure teams actually work

Modern platform and DevOps teams move fast, but traditional automation often doesn’t. Scripts fail. Deploy steps drift. One bad config can stall an entire pipeline.

Zrb-Flow is built to operate where that pain happens:

- **In the CLI**, where power users already work
- **With Docker**, for container-driven development and delivery
- **Alongside Kubernetes**, for orchestrated, production-grade environments
- **Across automation workflows**, where reliability matters more than hype

The result is a tool that feels native to technical teams and practical enough for real-world operations.

## Introducing Self-Healing Pipelines

One of the standout features in Zrb-Flow is **Self-Healing Pipelines**.

This capability helps detect broken scripts and automatically repair common failures before they turn into bigger delivery problems. When a pipeline step breaks, Zrb-Flow can analyze the issue, identify likely fixes, and get the workflow back on track.

That means less time digging through logs, less interruption during deploys, and less fragile automation overall.

For teams managing complex CI/CD processes, container builds, and cluster operations, Self-Healing Pipelines can be the difference between a stalled release and a smooth recovery.
